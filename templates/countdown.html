In the stillness, a whisper demanded we wait. The crown of beginnings sat upon no head. A bell rang, though no one held the rope. "Your time starts now," it said. We found envelopes scattered across the floor. Inside one: a potato, entirely unhelpful. Another: instructions to carry water in a sieve. We laughed, but the laugh echoed too long. A faint chalkboard appeared, tallying points we never earned. The air smelled faintly of confetti and shame. Shadows clapped politely at nothing in particular. "Fastest wins," the whisper chuckled.
